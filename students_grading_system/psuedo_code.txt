Students Grading System:
    How does  a real grading system work?
        1. What information does a school store for each student?
            1. Basic Student Information
                Identification Details: Student Name, unique Student ID number, and date of birth.
                Contact & Demographics: Address, guardian contact info (email, phone numbers), and emergency contacts.
            2. Academic & Enrollment Records
                Class & Schedule: Current grade level, homeroom teacher, and enrolled course list.
                Transcripts: Academic history, previously earned credits, and degrees or certificates pursued.
            3. Grading Details & Performance Metrics
                Coursework Grades: Scores from individual assignments, quizzes, projects, and midterms.
                Final Marks: End-of-term letter grades, percentages, or qualitative descriptions assigned to each class.
                Grade Point Average (GPA): Semester GPAs and Cumulative GPAs (CGPA) calculated based on grades and course credits
            4. Behavioral & Administrative Data
                Attendance: Daily attendance logs, tardiness records, and excused or unexcused absences.
                Conduct & Disciplinary Notes: Records of behavioral patterns, awards, or disciplinary actions.
            5. Health & Special Needs
                Medical Information: Immunization records, allergies, and nurse's office visits.
                Accommodations: Specialized education plans, such as Individualized Education Programs (IEP) or learning accommodations.
